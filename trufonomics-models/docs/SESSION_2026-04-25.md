## Honest findings worth preserving

1. **Path identifiability ≠ parameter identifiability** (Phase 1.2,
   1.5). Latent paths can be recovered to >99.9% Pearson while the
   underlying hyperparameters are non-identifiable up to a rescaling.
   Standard solutions (priors, post-hoc anchors) documented in each
   FINDINGS.

2. **Synthetic recovery validates the estimation core, not the model
   class fit to the target** (Phase 2.2). Our UC+SV+MS passed
   synthetic recovery cleanly, but on real BLS Headline CPI YoY the
   level layer absorbs all variance and the regime mechanism stays
   dormant. The fix is architectural (drop UC for already-differenced
   targets); the lesson is methodological (every archetype should be
   re-validated on its actual target before claiming production-ready).

3. **BSTS slope-drift question — empirically resolved**. LLT clearly
   wins on level series (CPI index), LL clearly wins on YoY series
   (Δ log-lik 8x in favor each way). Production rule: choose by
   transform, not theoretical preference.

4. **DFM scale identifiability** in hierarchical housing. The product
   `β·F` is identified; the absolute scale of either is not. Fix is a
   2-line post-hoc renormalization (β_NE := 1).
